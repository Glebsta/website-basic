Definitions and conventions:

The alternating spin chain of length \(2L\) is governed by the Hamiltonian $$\begin{eqnarray} {\mathbb H}&=&\frac{1}{\sin(2\gamma)}\ \sum_{m=1}^{2L}\, \Big(\,2\sin^2(\gamma)\, \sigma^z_{m\vphantom{+1}}\,\sigma^z_{m+1}- (\sigma^x_{m\vphantom{+1}}\, \sigma^x_{m+2}+\sigma^y_{m\vphantom{+1}}\,\sigma^y_{m+2}+ \sigma^z_{m\vphantom{+1}}\,\sigma^z_{m+2})\\[0.2cm] &+&{\rm i}\, (-1)^m\sin(\gamma)(\sigma^x_{m\vphantom{+1}}\,\sigma^y_{m+1\vphantom{+1}} -\sigma^y_{m\vphantom{+1}}\, \sigma^x_{m+1\vphantom{+1}})(\sigma^z_{m-1\vphantom{+1}}-\sigma^z_{m+2\vphantom{+1}}) \,\Big)+2L\,\cot(2\gamma)\ . \end{eqnarray}$$ Here the matrices \(\sigma_m^a\) with \((a=x,y,z)\) stand for the usual Pauli matrices acting on site \(m\). Instead of periodic boundary conditions, the quasi periodic boundary conditions are considered $$ \sigma^{\pm}_{2L+m}={\rm e}^{\pm 2{\rm i}\pi{\tt k}}\ \sigma^{\pm}_{m}\ , \qquad \sigma^{z}_{2L+m}=\sigma^{z}_{m\vphantom{+1}} \qquad \big(\sigma^\pm\equiv\tfrac{1}{2}\, (\sigma^x\pm{\rm i}\,\sigma^y)\,\big)\ , $$ with the parameter \({\tt k}\) lying within the ''first Brillouin zone'': $$ -\tfrac{1}{2}<{\tt k}\leq\tfrac{1}{2} \ . $$ The system, thus defined, turns out to be integrable. Its solution is obtained using the Bethe anstaz approach, and the corresponding equations read explicitly as $$ \bigg(\frac{\cosh(2\beta_j+{\rm i}\gamma)}{\cosh(2\beta_j-{\rm i}\gamma)}\bigg)^L= -{\rm e}^{-2{\rm i}\pi{\tt k}}\ \prod_{m=1}^M\frac{\sinh(\beta_j-\beta_m+{\rm i}\gamma)}{\sinh(\beta_j-\beta_m-{\rm i}\gamma)}\ . $$ The number of Bethe roots, \(M\), is related to the total spin, \(\frac{1}{2}\sum_j\sigma^z_j\), which is a conserved quantity for the chain $$M=L-S^z\ .$$
